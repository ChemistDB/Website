Daniel Barr

 
 

I grew up in the Harrisburg area in Pennsylvania, where I attended high school and became fascinated by the interactions of biological molecules and their role in the cell.  I went across country to the Barrett Honors College at Arizona State University for my undergraduate degree in Biochemistry with research in organic synthesis of photosynthetic compounds.  After a short project in Chemical Education that bridged my undergraduate and graduate careers, I moved into Computational Biophysical Chemistry for my doctoral work.  My research focuses on using computer simulations to gain insight into the mechanisms of conformational changes in biomolecules that enable them to perform a vast array of functions in the cell.  I am particularly interested learning how DNA-binding proteins recognize their specific binding sites and using this information to aid in the design of new small molecule drug candidates that bind DNA in a sequence-dependent manner.
